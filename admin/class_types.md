# Simultanous Reporting

Teams work on the task separately until 20mins before the end of class.  Each team then explains to the whole class their solution.

A list of team names with _one main takeaway_ from that team should be added to their log book by each student.`

# Lab Book/Log Book

Teams work together and each team member records as faithfully as they can the deliberations of their team and the outputs they created.

# Debating

Teams pair up on a problem, one takes the affermative and one takes the negative position.  After 40 minutess of preparation, the teams have a 10 minute debate and the crowd decides who won. 

Each student should record a list of team pairs and the _main takeaway they got from the debate_ between those two teams.

# Conference

This is not a team task.  Students register to speak prior to class and are given a time slot.  Each student presents their talk while the others respectfully listen. There is an opportunity to ask questions after each talk.  The class teacher acts as the session chair keeping everyone on time and asking a question if no one else does.

No log book entries are required for these classes.

# Competition

The task which is given includes a success metric.  At the end of the alloted time a leaderboard is written up showing which team did best.  Teams only, never rank individuals in this way.

Each individual student should record the final leaderboard in their lob books before leaving class.

# Butcher's Paper

The team task requires the creation of a diagram or a drawing.  Teams work on the task for 50 mins then the results are put up around the room.  Students get one star each to put on their favourite.

Each individual student whould copy their favourite answer into their log book.
